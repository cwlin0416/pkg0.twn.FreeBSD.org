FreeBSD.org pkg mirror

This is pkg0.twn.freebsd.org - an Asia regional mirror for pkg(8).

It is generously hosted by Department of Computer Science, National Chiao Tung University in Hsinchu, TW.

Beware, pkg(8) does not use http like your browser does. It does a DNS SRV record lookup and then processes the results according to weight and priority. It looks something like this:

% host -t srv _http._tcp.pkg.freebsd.org
_http._tcp.pkg.freebsd.org has SRV record 10 10 80 pkg0.twn.freebsd.org.
_http._tcp.pkg.freebsd.org has SRV record 20 10 80 pkg0.isc.freebsd.org.
_http._tcp.pkg.freebsd.org has SRV record 20 10 80 pkg0.nyi.freebsd.org.
_http._tcp.pkg.freebsd.org has SRV record 50 10 80 pkg0.bme.freebsd.org.
_http._tcp.pkg.freebsd.org has SRV record 50 10 80 pkg0.ydx.freebsd.org.

With this, it uses fallbacks in case the closest mirror is unavailable.

We use MaxMind GeoLite based geo-dns to attempt to bias towards the closest mirror for you.

The mirror your browser is viewing is PROBABLY the same one that pkg(8) would select but this is not guaranteed.
pkg(8) versions 1.1 and earlier do not implement priority selection so it is likey they will use a different mirror than this one.

IMPORTANT: We do NOT guarantee uptime of any particular mirror. We provide SRV fallbacks for redundancy.

If you configure your pkg client to use direct http access to a particular mirror and bypass the SRV fallback mechanism - or are behind a firewall that breaks DNS SRV records - you might experience occasional outages that do not affect clients that do use SRV records.

If your provider or gateway prevents you from using standard DNS SRV records, we encourage you to try to have this fixed.
